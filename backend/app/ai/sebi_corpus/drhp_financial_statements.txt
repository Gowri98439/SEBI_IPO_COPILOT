CHAPTER 1: KEY FINANCIAL RATIOS FOR SME IPO ANALYSIS
=============================================================================

1.1 PROFITABILITY RATIOS

EBITDA MARGIN:
EBITDA Margin (%) = (EBITDA / Net Revenue) × 100
EBITDA = Revenue from Operations - Raw Material Cost - Employee Costs - Other Operating Expenses
Benchmark (Manufacturing): 12-18% is considered healthy for SME manufacturing.
Benchmark (IT/Services): 18-25% is considered healthy for IT services.
Benchmark (NBFC): Not applicable (use NIM instead).

NET PROFIT MARGIN:
Net Profit Margin (%) = (PAT / Net Revenue) × 100
PAT = Profit After Tax (from audited P&L)
Benchmark (Manufacturing): 5-10% is typical for SME manufacturers.
Benchmark (IT/Services): 10-18% for IT services SMEs.
Red Flag: PAT margin below 2% for 3 consecutive years indicates weak fundamentals.
SEBI 2024 Amendment: Minimum PAT of Rs. 2 crore required in most recent year for SME IPO eligibility.

RETURN ON EQUITY (ROE):
ROE (%) = (PAT / Average Shareholders' Equity) × 100
Average Equity = (Opening Net Worth + Closing Net Worth) / 2
Benchmark: ROE > 15% is considered attractive for IPO investment.
Red Flag: ROE declining continuously over 3 years signals deteriorating capital efficiency.

RETURN ON CAPITAL EMPLOYED (ROCE):
ROCE (%) = (EBIT / Capital Employed) × 100
Capital Employed = Total Assets - Current Liabilities
Benchmark: ROCE > 15% indicates efficient capital deployment.
Note: ROCE should exceed the weighted average cost of capital (WACC) for value creation.

RETURN ON NET WORTH (RONW):
RONW (%) = (PAT / Net Worth) × 100
Net Worth = Paid-up Equity Share Capital + Reserves and Surplus
Required Disclosure: SEBI mandates RONW disclosure for each of the last 3 financial years in DRHP.

1.2 LIQUIDITY RATIOS

CURRENT RATIO:
Current Ratio = Current Assets / Current Liabilities
Benchmark: 1.5-2.0x is considered adequate. Below 1.0x indicates potential liquidity stress.
DRHP Disclosure: Should be disclosed for each of the last 3 financial years.

QUICK RATIO (ACID TEST):
Quick Ratio = (Cash + Short-term Investments + Receivables) / Current Liabilities
Benchmark: >1.0x is considered satisfactory. This excludes inventory which may not be quickly liquidated.

CASH CONVERSION CYCLE (CCC):
CCC = Debtor Days + Inventory Days - Creditor Days
Debtor Days = (Trade Receivables / Net Revenue) × 365
Inventory Days = (Inventory / COGS) × 365
Creditor Days = (Trade Payables / COGS) × 365
Red Flag: CCC exceeding 90 days for a manufacturing company indicates working capital inefficiency.

1.3 SOLVENCY RATIOS

DEBT TO EQUITY RATIO:
D/E Ratio = Total Debt / Shareholders' Equity
Total Debt = Long-term borrowings + Short-term borrowings + Current maturities of LTD
Benchmark (Manufacturing): D/E below 1.5x is generally considered manageable.
Red Flag: D/E above 3.0x signals high leverage risk.

INTEREST COVERAGE RATIO:
ICR = EBIT / Interest Expense
Benchmark: ICR > 3.0x indicates comfortable debt servicing ability.
Red Flag: ICR below 1.5x indicates potential debt servicing stress.

DEBT SERVICE COVERAGE RATIO (DSCR):
DSCR = (EBITDA - Taxes) / (Principal Repayment + Interest Payment)
Benchmark: DSCR > 1.25x is typically required by lenders.
Required Disclosure: DRHPs typically include projected DSCR for the period of utilisation of IPO proceeds.

1.4 VALUATION METRICS

EARNINGS PER SHARE (EPS):
Basic EPS = (PAT - Preference Dividend) / Weighted Average Equity Shares Outstanding
Diluted EPS = (PAT - Preference Dividend) / (WAESO + Potential Dilutive Equity)
Required Disclosure: Basic and Diluted EPS for each of the last 3 financial years.
Note: EPS must be computed on the face value basis and disclosed on pre-issue and post-issue basis.

PRICE-TO-EARNINGS RATIO (P/E):
P/E = Issue Price per Share / Basic EPS (most recent year)
Required Disclosure: P/E at issue price vs. Sector P/E and Peer P/E.
Note: Higher P/E than listed peers requires specific justification in the DRHP.

NET ASSET VALUE (NAV) PER SHARE:
NAV = Net Worth / Total Number of Equity Shares Outstanding
Required Disclosure: NAV as at date of last audited balance sheet and post-issue.
Note: Issue price at a significant premium to NAV must be justified by demonstrated earnings growth.

PRICE-TO-BOOK VALUE (P/BV):
P/BV = Issue Price / NAV per share
Benchmark: P/BV of 1.5-3.0x is typical for SME IPOs. Higher ratios require strong growth justification.

CHAPTER 2: IND AS FINANCIAL STATEMENT STRUCTURE FOR DRHP
=============================================================================

2.1 STATEMENT OF PROFIT AND LOSS (P&L) — REQUIRED FORMAT

Revenue from Operations:
- Sale of Products (Net of Taxes)
- Sale of Services
- Other Operating Revenue
= Total Revenue from Operations (Net)

Other Income:
- Interest Income
- Dividend Income
- Profit on Sale of Investments/Assets
- Miscellaneous Income
= Total Other Income

Total Income = Revenue from Operations + Other Income

Expenses:
- Cost of Materials Consumed / Raw Materials + Packing Materials
- Purchases of Stock-in-Trade
- Changes in Inventories (Opening minus Closing)
- Employee Benefits Expense (Salaries, PF, Gratuity, ESOP)
- Finance Costs (Interest, Processing Fees, Bank Charges)
- Depreciation and Amortisation Expense
- Other Expenses (Power, Freight, Repairs, Marketing, Admin)
= Total Expenses

Profit Before Exceptional Items and Tax (PBEITAT)
Exceptional Items (with disclosure of nature)
Profit Before Tax (PBT)
Tax Expense:
- Current Tax (MAT or normal)
- Deferred Tax (Credit)/Charge
- Tax for Earlier Years
= Total Tax Expense
Profit After Tax (PAT)
Other Comprehensive Income (OCI):
- Remeasurement of Defined Benefit Plans (net of tax)
- Fair Value Changes on Financial Instruments (FVOCI)
= Total OCI
Total Comprehensive Income

2.2 BALANCE SHEET — REQUIRED FORMAT (IND AS)

ASSETS:
Non-Current Assets:
- Property, Plant and Equipment (PPE) — Net Block
- Capital Work in Progress (CWIP)
- Right-of-Use Assets (Lease Assets under Ind AS 116)
- Goodwill and Other Intangible Assets
- Financial Assets: Investments (LT), Loans (LT), Other Financial Assets (LT)
- Deferred Tax Assets (Net)
- Other Non-Current Assets

Current Assets:
- Inventories (Raw Materials, WIP, Finished Goods, Consumables)
- Financial Assets: Investments (ST), Trade Receivables, Cash and Cash Equivalents, Bank Balances, Loans (ST)
- Current Tax Assets (Net)
- Other Current Assets (Prepaid Expenses, GST Receivable, Advances to Suppliers)

Total Assets = Non-Current Assets + Current Assets

EQUITY AND LIABILITIES:
Equity:
- Equity Share Capital (Face Value × Shares Outstanding)
- Other Equity (Reserves and Surplus):
  - Capital Reserve
  - Securities Premium Reserve
  - General Reserve
  - Retained Earnings
  - Other Components of Equity (FVOCI Reserve, Remeasurement Reserve)

Non-Current Liabilities:
- Financial Liabilities: Borrowings (LT), Lease Liabilities (LT)
- Provisions (Gratuity, Leave Encashment — LT portion)
- Deferred Tax Liabilities (Net)

Current Liabilities:
- Financial Liabilities: Borrowings (ST), Current Maturities of LT Debt, Trade Payables (MSME and Others), Lease Liabilities (ST)
- Other Current Liabilities (Statutory Dues, Advance from Customers, Employee Payables)
- Provisions (Gratuity, Leave — Current portion)
- Current Tax Liabilities (Net)

Total Equity and Liabilities = Equity + Non-Current Liabilities + Current Liabilities

2.3 CASH FLOW STATEMENT — INDIRECT METHOD

Operating Cash Flow (OCF):
+ PAT
+ Depreciation and Amortisation
+ Finance Costs (to be added back)
- Interest Income (to be deducted)
+/- Working Capital Changes:
  (Increase)/Decrease in Trade Receivables
  (Increase)/Decrease in Inventories
  (Increase)/Decrease in Other Current Assets
  Increase/(Decrease) in Trade Payables
  Increase/(Decrease) in Other Current Liabilities
- Taxes Paid (Net of Refund)
= Net Cash from Operating Activities (A)

Investing Cash Flow:
- Capital Expenditure (Purchase of PPE and Intangibles)
- Investment in Subsidiaries/Associates
+ Proceeds from Sale of PPE
+ Proceeds from Sale of Investments
+/- (Increase)/Decrease in Other Financial Assets
= Net Cash from Investing Activities (B)

Financing Cash Flow:
+ Proceeds from Long-term Borrowings
- Repayment of Long-term Borrowings
+/- Net Change in Short-term Borrowings
- Finance Costs Paid
- Dividend Paid (if any)
+ Proceeds from Issue of Share Capital (Rights, ESOP etc.)
= Net Cash from Financing Activities (C)

Net Increase/(Decrease) in Cash = A + B + C
Add: Opening Cash and Cash Equivalents
Closing Cash and Cash Equivalents

CHAPTER 3: FINANCIAL DUE DILIGENCE RED FLAGS IN DRHP REVIEW
=============================================================================

3.1 REVENUE QUALITY RED FLAGS
- Revenue growing significantly faster than cash from operations (suggests aggressive recognition)
- Large debtors from related parties (potential circular transactions)
- Revenue from a single customer exceeding 30% (concentration risk)
- Sudden revenue spike in final year before IPO (window dressing suspicion)
- GST filing revenues inconsistent with stated revenues
- Debtors Days continuously increasing (collection difficulty)

3.2 PROFITABILITY RED FLAGS
- Margins significantly higher than industry peers without clear explanation
- EBITDA margin improvement driven by cost reduction rather than revenue growth
- One-time income inflating PBT/PAT in the qualifying year
- Exceptional items that recur every year (not truly exceptional)
- Operating expense trends inconsistent with revenue trend

3.3 BALANCE SHEET RED FLAGS
- Advances to related parties classified under Other Current Assets
- Intangible assets capitalized aggressively (goodwill on internal reorganization)
- Capital Work in Progress (CWIP) stagnant for years (non-productive capex)
- Inventory write-down provision inadequate relative to aging
- Contingent liabilities not adequately disclosed

3.4 CASH FLOW RED FLAGS
- PAT significantly higher than OCF (indicates poor earnings quality)
- Heavy reliance on short-term borrowings for long-term assets
- Capex much lower than stated depreciation for three years (asset sweating)
- Negative free cash flow every year despite claimed profitability
- Financing cash flow heavily positive from borrowings not reflected in asset growth

CHAPTER 4: FIVE-YEAR FINANCIAL TREND ANALYSIS FOR DRHP
=============================================================================

4.1 REVENUE CAGR BENCHMARKS
Excellent: Revenue CAGR > 25% over 5 years
Good: Revenue CAGR 15-25%
Average: Revenue CAGR 8-15%
Below Average: Revenue CAGR < 8%

4.2 PAT GROWTH BENCHMARKS
Excellent: PAT CAGR > 30%
Good: PAT CAGR 20-30%
Average: PAT CAGR 10-20%
Below Average: PAT CAGR < 10%

4.3 TYPICAL SME IPO FINANCIAL PROFILE (MANUFACTURING SECTOR):
Revenue: Rs. 30-150 crore
EBITDA: Rs. 5-25 crore
PAT: Rs. 2-15 crore
Net Worth: Rs. 15-80 crore
Total Debt: Rs. 10-60 crore
D/E Ratio: 0.5x - 2.0x
EBITDA Margin: 12-18%
PAT Margin: 5-10%
ROE: 12-20%

4.4 TYPICAL SME IPO FINANCIAL PROFILE (IT SERVICES):
Revenue: Rs. 20-80 crore
EBITDA: Rs. 4-20 crore
PAT: Rs. 2-15 crore
Net Worth: Rs. 10-50 crore
Total Debt: Rs. 2-20 crore (asset-light, lower debt)
D/E Ratio: 0.1x - 0.5x
EBITDA Margin: 18-28%
PAT Margin: 10-20%
ROE: 18-30%

4.5 TYPICAL SME IPO FINANCIAL PROFILE (NBFC):
Loan Book: Rs. 50-500 crore
NIM (Net Interest Margin): 4-8%
Gross NPA: < 3% (preferred), 3-6% (acceptable), >6% (red flag)
CAR (Capital Adequacy Ratio): > 15% (RBI minimum: 15% for NBFC-ND-SI)
PAT: Rs. 3-25 crore
ROA (Return on Assets): 1.5-3.5%
ROE: 12-18%

CHAPTER 5: OBJECTS OF THE ISSUE — STANDARD STRUCTURE
=============================================================================

5.1 COMMON IPO OBJECTS:
1. CAPITAL EXPENDITURE: Purchase of plant, machinery, equipment, technology
   - Must include detailed item-wise list with quotes obtained
   - Must specify vendor names (where finalized)
   - Must include installation/commissioning timeline
   
2. WORKING CAPITAL REQUIREMENT:
   - Bankers' assessment of incremental working capital
   - Working capital computation to be certified by CA
   - Net working capital = Current Assets (excluding cash) - Current Liabilities (excluding short-term bank borrowings)
   
3. DEBT REPAYMENT:
   - Specific loans/facilities to be repaid must be named
   - Outstanding amount as at a recent date must be stated
   - Cannot exceed total outstanding debt as at filing date
   
4. GENERAL CORPORATE PURPOSE:
   - Cannot exceed 25% of total issue proceeds
   - SEBI does not allow "other general corporate purposes" exceeding this limit
   - Must have some general description of intended use

5.2 DEPLOYMENT SCHEDULE:
Year 1: X% of funds (amount in Rs. crore)
Year 2: Y% of funds
Year 3: Z% of funds (if applicable)
Total = 100%

5.3 BRIDGE FINANCING DISCLOSURE:
If any of the IPO objects have been funded through bridge loans or internal accruals pending IPO, this must be disclosed with the amount, lender name, and repayment schedule.
