SECTION 2: INDUSTRY OVERVIEW — BY SECTOR

2.1 INDIAN MANUFACTURING SECTOR

MACRO OVERVIEW (2024-2025):
India's manufacturing sector contributes approximately 16-17% of GDP and is poised to grow to 25% under the government's 'Make in India' and Production Linked Incentive (PLI) scheme initiatives. The sector employs over 27% of India's workforce. India's manufacturing PMI has consistently remained above 50 (expansion territory) for 36+ consecutive months through 2024.

Key growth drivers:
- Government's PLI schemes covering 14 sectors with Rs. 1.97 lakh crore incentives
- China+1 strategy creating export opportunities for Indian manufacturers
- Infrastructure spending (Rs. 11.11 lakh crore capex in Union Budget 2024-25)
- Formalization of supply chains post-GST
- Increasing domestic consumption with rising middle class

KEY SUB-SECTORS (Size and Growth):
Engineering Goods: Rs. 6.2 lakh crore (export: Rs. 1.11 lakh crore), growing at 8-10% CAGR
Textiles and Apparel: Rs. 13 lakh crore industry size, 12% CAGR targeted
Chemicals: Rs. 9 lakh crore, growing at 9-11% CAGR
Auto Components: Rs. 5.6 lakh crore, growing at 12-14% CAGR
Food Processing: Rs. 30 lakh crore, 11.18% CAGR

2.2 INFORMATION TECHNOLOGY AND IT-ENABLED SERVICES

MACRO OVERVIEW (2024-2025):
India's IT-BPM sector revenues reached USD 254 billion in FY 2024 (NASSCOM), making India the world's largest IT services exporter. The sector employs 5.4 million professionals directly and 13 million indirectly. India holds ~55% of the global IT outsourcing market share.

Key growth drivers:
- Global digital transformation spending projected at USD 2.3 trillion by 2025
- Generative AI adoption creating new demand for AI/ML services
- Cloud migration (SaaS, PaaS, IaaS) continuing across all industries
- India's Digital India initiative creating domestic IT demand
- Startup ecosystem generating IP creation and product companies

Key Sub-sectors:
Tier-1 IT Services (TCS, Infosys, HCL): USD 150+ billion combined revenues
Mid-tier IT companies: USD 2-5 billion revenues each
SME IT companies: Revenue Rs. 5-500 crore, high growth trajectory
ITES/BPO: USD 40 billion sector, growing at 7-9% CAGR
Product companies (SaaS): Fastest growing at 25-35% CAGR

2.3 FAST MOVING CONSUMER GOODS (FMCG)

MACRO OVERVIEW (2024-2025):
India's FMCG industry is valued at USD 110 billion (Rs. 9.2 lakh crore) as of 2024 and is projected to reach USD 220 billion by 2025 (IBEF). It is the fourth largest sector in India. Urban and rural markets are growing at different rates, with rural growth accelerating due to government welfare schemes and improved distribution.

Key growth drivers:
- India's population of 1.4 billion with growing per-capita income
- Rapid urbanization — 40% urban by 2030 (from ~35% today)
- Rural demand recovery driven by normal monsoon and MNREGA wage growth
- Quick Commerce (Q-commerce) growth — 10-15 minute delivery disrupting traditional FMCG
- Health and wellness trend driving premiumization
- Modern trade (supermarkets, hypermarkets) expanding rapidly

Key Sub-sectors:
Food and Beverages: ~50% of FMCG market
Personal Care: ~20% of market
Healthcare/OTC Pharma: ~15% of market
Homecare: ~15% of market

2.4 HEALTHCARE AND PHARMACEUTICALS

MACRO OVERVIEW (2024-2025):
India's healthcare industry (hospitals + pharma + diagnostics + medical devices) is valued at USD 372 billion (2024) and is growing at 16% CAGR. India is the 3rd largest pharmaceutical market globally by volume and 14th by value. India exports pharmaceuticals to 200+ countries.

Key growth drivers:
- Government's Ayushman Bharat Yojana covering 50 crore beneficiaries
- National Medical Devices Policy 2023 targeting USD 25 billion export by 2030
- India's ageing population driving healthcare demand
- Growing health insurance penetration
- Generic pharmaceutical leadership globally

Indian Pharma:
Rs. 4.6 lakh crore domestic + export market combined
Export market: USD 25.3 billion (FY 2023-24)
Generic drugs: India produces 20% of global generic medicines
Bulk drugs: India supplies 57% of vaccines globally

2.5 REAL ESTATE AND CONSTRUCTION

MACRO OVERVIEW (2024-2025):
India's real estate sector is the second largest employer after agriculture and is projected to reach USD 1 trillion by 2030 (from USD 477 billion in 2022). Housing demand is driven by urbanization, nuclear family formation, and government affordable housing schemes (PMAY).

Key growth drivers:
- PM Awas Yojana (Urban): 10 million houses targeted
- Smart Cities Mission: 100 cities with Rs. 2 lakh crore investment
- Commercial real estate demand from GCC (Global Capability Centres) and data centers
- Data Center capacity in India to triple to 1.7 GW by 2027
- Infrastructure upgradation (airports, highways, ports)

2.6 NBFC AND FINTECH SECTOR

MACRO OVERVIEW (2024-2025):
India's NBFC sector has a loan book of Rs. 37+ lakh crore (as of March 2024). NBFCs serve credit-underserved segments including MSMEs, rural households, and self-employed individuals. India has 9,500+ registered NBFCs as of 2024.

Key growth drivers:
- MSME credit gap of USD 530 billion (IMC Chamber study)
- Financial inclusion push: Jan Dhan + Aadhaar + Mobile (JAM Trinity)
- Digital lending growth through Account Aggregator framework
- Co-lending with banks under RBI co-lending guidelines
- UPI-based credit (credit on UPI) enabling micro-credit

Fintech sub-sector:
India's fintech market size: USD 111 billion (2024), projected USD 421 billion by 2029
Payment fintech: Dominated by PhonePe, Paytm, GPay
Lending fintech: Rs. 2.5 lakh crore digital loan book
Insurtech: Growing at 30%+ CAGR
Wealthtech: Rs. 1 lakh crore AUM managed by digital wealth platforms

2.7 LOGISTICS AND SUPPLY CHAIN

MACRO OVERVIEW (2024-2025):
India's logistics sector is valued at Rs. 14.4 lakh crore (USD 180 billion) and is projected to reach USD 380 billion by 2025. India's logistics cost as % of GDP has reduced from 14% to 9% post-GST and National Logistics Policy. India ranks 38th on World Bank's Logistics Performance Index (2023).

Key growth drivers:
- National Logistics Policy 2022 targeting logistics cost <8% of GDP
- PM GatiShakti National Master Plan — multi-modal logistics
- Dedicated Freight Corridors (Eastern and Western) becoming operational
- E-commerce growth driving last-mile delivery demand
- Cold chain infrastructure expansion for pharma and food

2.8 RENEWABLE ENERGY SECTOR

MACRO OVERVIEW (2024-2025):
India is targeting 500 GW renewable energy capacity by 2030, up from 190 GW installed as of March 2024. India ranks 4th globally in renewable energy capacity. The government's PLI scheme for solar modules, batteries, and electrolysers is driving domestic manufacturing.

Key growth drivers:
- India's NDC commitment: 50% power from renewables by 2030
- Falling solar tariffs — now competitive with thermal power
- Green Hydrogen Mission: Rs. 19,744 crore for development
- EV adoption creating battery storage demand
- Corporate PPAs from large corporates seeking green energy

SECTION 3: KEY REGULATORY AND POLICY ENVIRONMENT
=============================================================================

3.1 MSME SECTOR SUPPORT POLICIES:
- MSME Definition: Micro (upto Rs. 1 Cr investment, Rs. 5 Cr turnover), Small (upto Rs. 10 Cr, Rs. 50 Cr), Medium (upto Rs. 50 Cr, Rs. 250 Cr) — as per MSMED Act Amendment 2020
- MSME Emergency Credit Line Guarantee Scheme (ECLGS) — Rs. 5 lakh crore
- MSME Udyam Registration Portal — 5 crore+ registrations
- Priority Sector Lending (PSL) mandate — 7.5% of ANBC for MSMEs
- Government e-Marketplace (GeM) — Rs. 4 lakh crore procurement from MSMEs

3.2 EXPORT PROMOTION:
- Production Linked Incentive (PLI) Scheme — 14 sectors, Rs. 1.97 lakh crore
- Remission of Duties and Taxes on Exported Products (RoDTEP) scheme
- Duty Free Import Authorisation (DFIA) for export-linked inputs
- SEBI's IFSC Gateway for cross-border capital market access

3.3 FOREIGN DIRECT INVESTMENT (FDI):
- 100% FDI via automatic route in most sectors
- FDI Inflows to India: USD 71 billion (FY 2023-24)
- Key receiving sectors: Services, Computer Software, Telecom, Construction, Pharmaceuticals
