SEBI ICDR (ISSUE OF CAPITAL AND DISCLOSURE REQUIREMENTS) REGULATIONS, 2018
RELEVANT PROVISIONS FOR SME IPO OFFER DOCUMENT PREPARATION

================================================================================
CHAPTER I — PRELIMINARY
================================================================================

Regulation 1: Short title and commencement
(1) These regulations may be called the Securities and Exchange Board of India (Issue of Capital and Disclosure Requirements) Regulations, 2018.
(2) They shall come into force on the date of their publication in the Official Gazette.

Regulation 2: Definitions
(1) In these regulations, unless the context otherwise requires —
(a) "Act" means the Securities and Exchange Board of India Act, 1992 (15 of 1992);
(b) "allotment" means transfer of securities by an issuer to an applicant pursuant to an issue;
(c) "application supported by blocked amount" or "ASBA" means an application for subscription to an issue, whether public or rights, along with an authorisation to Self Certified Syndicate Bank to block the application money in the bank account maintained with SCSB;
(d) "board" means the Securities and Exchange Board of India established under section 3 of the Act;
(e) "book building" means a process undertaken to elicit demand and to assess the price for determination of the quantum or value of specified securities, as specified in Schedule XI of these regulations;
(f) "capital" with its grammatical variations means equity shares, convertible preference shares, convertible debentures or any other security which would be convertible into or exchangeable with equity shares at a later date;
(g) "company" means a company as defined in section 2(20) of the Companies Act, 2013;
(h) "convertible security" means a security which is convertible into or exchangeable with equity shares of the issuer at a later date, with or without the option of the holder of the security, and includes convertible debt instrument and convertible preference shares;
(i) "draft offer document" means a draft prospectus or draft letter of offer filed with the Board for observations;
(j) "eligible securities" means equity shares and convertible securities as specified in Schedule I of these regulations;
(k) "equity share capital" means all fully paid equity shares of an issuer;
(l) "firm allotment" means an allotment of securities, on a firm basis, to specified categories of persons, as specified in these regulations;
(m) "general information document" means a document as specified by the Board, containing important information that is common to all issuers proposing to undertake a public issue;
(n) "issuer" means any person making an offer of specified securities to the public or to the holders of the issuer's specified securities;
(o) "market maker" means a stock broker who quotes both buy and sell prices in a financial instrument or commodity held in inventory;
(p) "net worth" has the same meaning assigned to it under the Companies Act, 2013;
(q) "offer document" means prospectus in case of a public issue or offer for sale, and letter of offer in case of a rights issue;
(r) "price band" means the band within which the issue price can be determined for book built issue;
(s) "promoter" has the same meaning assigned to it under the Act;
(t) "promoter group" means the promoter group as specified in Schedule II of these regulations;
(u) "public issue" means an initial public offer or a further public offer;
(v) "qualified institutional buyer" or "QIB" means a mutual fund, foreign portfolio investor other than individuals, sub-accounts and foreign corporates registered with SEBI; domestic venture capital funds registered with SEBI; alternative investment funds registered with SEBI; foreign venture capital investors registered with SEBI; scheduled commercial banks; state industrial development corporations; insurance companies registered with the Insurance Regulatory and Development Authority; provident funds with minimum corpus of rupees twenty five crore; pension funds with minimum corpus of rupees twenty five crore; National Investment Fund set up by resolution no. F. No. 2/3/2005-DDII dated November 23, 2005 of the Government of India published in the Gazette of India; insurance funds set up and managed by army, navy or air force of the Union of India; insurance funds set up and managed by the Department of Posts, India; systemically important non-banking financial companies;
(w) "red herring prospectus" means a prospectus, which does not have complete particulars of the price at which the equity shares are being offered and the total number of equity shares to be offered;
(x) "registrar" means the registrar to an issue as defined under the Securities and Exchange Board of India (Registrars to an Issue and Share Transfer Agents) Regulations, 1993;
(y) "rights issue" means an offer of specified securities by an issuer to the shareholders of the issuer as on the record date fixed for the said purpose;
(z) "SME exchange" means a trading platform of a recognised stock exchange having nationwide trading terminals permitted by the Board to list the specified securities issued in accordance with Chapter IX of these regulations and includes a platform of a stock exchange, if any, permitted by the Board to list such specified securities;
(za) "specified securities" means equity shares and convertible securities;
(zb) "sponsor bank" means a Scheduled Commercial Bank appointed by the issuer to act as the sponsor of the ASBA process of the issuer;
(zc) "syndicate member" means an intermediary registered with the Board and who is permitted to carry on activity as an underwriter;
(zd) "underwriting" means an agreement by the underwriter to subscribe to or purchase or to procure subscribers to or purchasers for specified securities offered for sale to the public;
(ze) "working days" means all days on which commercial banks in Mumbai are open for business.

CHAPTER II — ELIGIBILITY REQUIREMENTS FOR PUBLIC ISSUE
================================================================================

Regulation 5: Conditions for public issue — General
(1) An issuer may make a public issue of specified securities, if it satisfies the following conditions:
(a) the issuer has net tangible assets of at least three crore rupees in each of the preceding three full years (of twelve months each), of which not more than fifty percent are held in monetary assets;
(b) the issuer has a minimum average pre-tax operating profit of fifteen crore rupees, calculated on a restated and consolidated basis, during the three most profitable years out of the immediately preceding five years;
(c) the issuer has a net worth of at least one crore rupees in each of the preceding three full years;
(d) the aggregate of the proposed issue and all previous issues made in the same financial year in terms of issue size does not exceed five times the pre-issue net worth of the issuer as per the audited balance sheet of the preceding financial year;
(e) if the issuer has changed its name within the last one year, at least fifty percent of the revenue for the preceding one full year has been earned by the issuer from the activity indicated by the new name.
(2) An issuer not satisfying the conditions stated in sub-regulation (1) may make a public issue only through the book building process, subject to the condition that:
(a) the issuer undertakes to allot at least seventy five percent of the net offer to qualified institutional buyers and to refund the full subscription monies if it fails to make the said minimum allotment to qualified institutional buyers;
(b) the issuer has a pre-issue net worth of not less than one crore rupees in the preceding three years.

CHAPTER IX — ISSUE OF SPECIFIED SECURITIES BY SMALL AND MEDIUM ENTERPRISES
================================================================================

Regulation 229: Applicability
(1) The provisions of this Chapter shall apply to an issue of specified securities by a small and medium enterprise.
(2) The provisions of Chapter II, III, IV, V, VI, VII, VIII of these regulations shall not apply to an issue made under this Chapter, unless otherwise specified.
(3) For the purposes of this Chapter, an issuer shall be considered to be a small and medium enterprise if:
(a) the post-issue paid up capital of the issuer shall not exceed rupees twenty-five crores; or
(b) the issuer satisfies such other conditions as may be specified by the Board from time to time.

Regulation 230: Eligibility requirements for SME IPO
(1) An issuer may make an initial public offer on a SME exchange, if it satisfies the following conditions:
(a) the issuer shall have been incorporated under the Companies Act, 2013 or the Companies Act, 1956 and have a track record of at least three years;
(b) the issuer shall have tangible assets of at least one crore and fifty lakhs rupees as per the latest audited financial results;
(c) the post-issue paid-up capital of the issuer shall not exceed rupees twenty-five crore;
(d) the issuer shall have a positive net worth as per the latest audited financial results;
(e) the issuer shall have a track record of distributable profits in terms of section 123 of the Companies Act, 2013, for at least two years out of immediately preceding three years, at the time of filing of the offer document with the stock exchange;
(f) Provided that in case the issuer does not have a track record of distributable profits for two out of three years, it shall mandatorily appoint a market maker.
(g) the issuer shall not have any winding up petition admitted by a court or tribunal of competent jurisdiction against it which is pending;
(h) the promoters/directors shall not be debarred from accessing capital markets by the Board;
(i) the issuer shall not be a wilful defaulter as defined by Reserve Bank of India;
(j) the application and allotment process shall be in electronic mode only.

Regulation 231: Minimum Promoters' Contribution
(1) The promoters of the issuer shall hold at least twenty percent of the post-issue paid-up equity share capital.
(2) The promoters' contribution shall be brought in upfront before the opening of the issue.
(3) An alternative investment fund may be treated as a promoter for the purpose of these regulations subject to the conditions specified by the Board.

Regulation 232: Lock-in of Promoters' Contribution
(1) The minimum promoters' contribution as specified in Regulation 231 shall be locked-in for a period of three years from the date of commencement of commercial production or from the date of allotment in the public issue, whichever is later.
(2) The promoters' contribution in excess of minimum promoters' contribution shall be locked-in for a period of one year from the date of allotment in the public issue.
(3) The securities held by the promoters and offered for sale in the issue shall not be subject to lock-in after the issue.
(4) The lock-in period shall commence from the date of allotment in the public issue.

Regulation 233: Pre-issue obligations for SME IPO
(1) The issuer shall enter into an agreement with the SME exchange for listing of its specified securities.
(2) The issuer shall appoint a SEBI registered merchant banker as lead manager to the issue.
(3) The issuer shall appoint a SEBI registered registrar to the issue as registrar.
(4) The issuer shall appoint a market maker registered with the SME exchange for a minimum period of three years.
(5) The lead manager shall exercise due diligence and ensure compliance of all requirements under these regulations.
(6) The offer document shall be filed with the stock exchange where the specified securities are proposed to be listed.
(7) The offer document shall be made available on the websites of the stock exchange and the issuer.

Regulation 234: Contents of Offer Document for SME IPO
(1) The offer document for SME IPO shall contain the following:
(a) Cover Page disclosures:
    (i) Name of the issuer and registered office address
    (ii) Details of the public issue: fresh issue, offer for sale or combination
    (iii) Price band or issue price
    (iv) Issue opening and closing dates
    (v) Name and contact details of the lead manager(s)
    (vi) Name and contact details of the registrar to the issue
    (vii) Risk factor summary (top 5 material risk factors)

(b) General Information:
    (i) Name, address, registration number and website of the issuer
    (ii) Names and designations of the directors
    (iii) Names of key managerial personnel
    (iv) Roles and responsibilities of the lead manager
    (v) Details of the market maker

(c) Capital Structure:
    (i) Details of the authorized, issued, subscribed and paid-up capital
    (ii) History of share capital of the issuer
    (iii) Equity share capital history: date of allotment, number of shares, face value, issue price, nature of consideration
    (iv) Details of promoters' contribution and lock-in
    (v) Pre-issue and post-issue shareholding pattern

(d) Objects of the Issue:
    (i) Statement of objects of the issue
    (ii) Break-up of the objects: specific purpose-wise utilization
    (iii) Schedule of implementation
    (iv) Means of finance: amount proposed to be funded from internal accruals
    (v) Appraisal by bank or financial institution, if any

(e) Basis for Issue Price:
    (i) Earnings per share (EPS) for last three financial years
    (ii) Price to earnings ratio
    (iii) Comparison of EPS and P/E of peer companies
    (iv) Return on net worth for last three years
    (v) Net asset value per share based on last audited balance sheet

(f) Statement of Tax Benefits:
    (i) Tax benefits available to the issuer
    (ii) Tax benefits available to the shareholders

(g) About the Company — Industry and Business Overview:
    (i) Industry overview with statistical data
    (ii) Business overview: nature of business, products/services
    (iii) Key competitive strengths
    (iv) Business strategy
    (v) Description of properties
    (vi) Key raw materials and suppliers
    (vii) Key customers and marketing strategy
    (viii) Details of orders/projects in hand

(h) Management Discussion and Analysis:
    (i) Overview of the financial performance
    (ii) Factors affecting results of operations
    (iii) Discussion of financial condition: revenues, expenditures, profitability
    (iv) Comparison of recent financial years
    (v) Unusual or infrequent events that have had material effect
    (vi) Significant economic changes that materially affected income
    (vii) Known trends or uncertainties
    (viii) Future relationship between costs and income
    (ix) Reasons for material changes in net sales or revenues
    (x) Extent of seasonality in business operations

(i) Risk Factors:
    (i) Internal risk factors: business, financial, operational, managerial
    (ii) External risk factors: industry, regulatory, economic
    (iii) Risk factors specific to the issue
    (iv) Risks related to equity shares
    Note: Each risk factor shall be accompanied by proposed mitigation measures.

(j) Directors and Key Managerial Personnel:
    (i) Brief profiles of all directors
    (ii) Qualifications and experience of directors
    (iii) Details of other directorships held
    (iv) Brief profiles of key managerial personnel
    (v) Payment of compensation to directors and KMP
    (vi) Interests of directors in the issuer
    (vii) Changes in the Board of Directors in last three years

(k) Promoters and Promoter Group:
    (i) Brief profiles of promoters
    (ii) Shareholding of promoters in the issuer
    (iii) Related party transactions with the issuer
    (iv) Litigations pending against promoters
    (v) Promoters' business experience

(l) Financial Information:
    (i) Audited financial statements for last three financial years
    (ii) Restated financial statements as per Schedule III
    (iii) Management letter and auditor's report
    (iv) Changes in accounting policies
    (v) Related party disclosures
    (vi) Segment-wise reporting if applicable
    (vii) Summary of audited financial statements

(m) Legal and Other Information:
    (i) Outstanding litigations and defaults
    (ii) Material contracts and inspection of documents
    (iii) Approvals and licenses obtained
    (iv) Regulatory approvals required but not yet obtained
    (v) Patents, trademarks and intellectual property

(n) Other Regulatory and Statutory Disclosures:
    (i) Declaration from lead manager regarding compliance
    (ii) Expert opinions
    (iii) Changes in auditors in last three years
    (iv) Declaration of compliance with corporate governance requirements

Regulation 235: Pricing of SME IPO
(1) An issuer may determine the issue price of the specified securities in consultation with the lead manager.
(2) The issue price shall be determined through book building process or at a fixed price.
(3) If the issue is through book building, a price band of not more than twenty percent above the floor price shall be established.
(4) The minimum subscription amount per application shall be rupees one lakh.
(5) The issue size shall not be less than rupees fifty lakhs and shall not exceed rupees twenty-five crore.

Regulation 236: Allotment in SME IPO
(1) The allotment in an SME IPO shall be made in minimum trading lot size as determined by the SME exchange.
(2) The minimum application amount shall be the minimum trading lot as determined by the stock exchange.
(3) The issuer shall ensure that at least fifty percent of the net offer is allotted to retail individual investors.
(4) In case of oversubscription, allotment shall be made on proportionate basis within each category.

Regulation 237: Market Making Requirements
(1) The issuer shall mandatorily appoint a market maker registered with the SME exchange.
(2) The market maker shall provide two-way quotes continuously on the SME exchange for a minimum period of three years from the date of listing.
(3) The market maker inventory shall not be less than five percent of the issue size.
(4) The market maker shall enter into an agreement with the issuer specifying the scope of market making activities.
(5) The spread between the bid and ask price shall not exceed the limits specified by the SME exchange.

Regulation 238: Post-issue obligations for SME IPO
(1) After the closing of the issue, the issuer shall:
(a) complete allotment of specified securities within six working days from the closure of the issue;
(b) make listing application to the SME exchange within the prescribed timeline;
(c) ensure refund of application money to unsuccessful applicants within the prescribed timeline;
(d) submit post-issue monitoring report to SEBI within the prescribed period;
(e) submit statement of utilization of issue proceeds to the audit committee on a quarterly basis.

CHAPTER X — ADDITIONAL DISCLOSURE REQUIREMENTS
================================================================================

Regulation 239: Disclosures regarding promoters
(1) The offer document shall disclose the following regarding promoters:
(a) Names and addresses of all promoters
(b) Percentage of shareholding of each promoter
(c) Number of shares pledged by each promoter
(d) Experience in the business/industry
(e) Other ventures of the promoters
(f) Any criminal proceedings pending against promoters
(g) Any pending SEBI action against promoters
(h) Any winding up proceedings against any entity controlled by promoters

Regulation 240: Disclosures regarding litigation
(1) The offer document shall disclose all outstanding litigation against the issuer, promoters, directors and group companies involving:
(a) Criminal proceedings
(b) Tax proceedings with amount exceeding 1% of the net worth
(c) Matters before regulatory authorities
(d) Arbitration proceedings
(e) Civil suits with amount exceeding 1% of the net worth
(f) Any litigations involving the promoters or directors that may affect the issuer

Regulation 241: Disclosures regarding related party transactions
(1) The offer document shall disclose all related party transactions entered into by the issuer as per Accounting Standard 18 or Indian Accounting Standard 24, as applicable.
(2) The disclosure shall include:
(a) Nature of related party relationship
(b) Transaction amounts for each of the past three years
(c) Outstanding balances at the end of each year
(d) Terms and conditions of the transactions
(e) Details of any guarantees given to related parties

Regulation 242: Financial disclosures and auditor reports
(1) The financial statements included in the offer document shall be:
(a) Prepared in accordance with applicable accounting standards
(b) Audited by a statutory auditor of the issuer who is a member of the Institute of Chartered Accountants of India
(c) Restated as per the Schedule III of these regulations
(d) For a period of at least three financial years preceding the filing of the offer document
(2) The auditor shall issue a report on restated financial statements confirming compliance.
(3) The offer document shall include a statement on adjustments made to financial statements during restatement.
(4) An updated audited financial statement shall be included if the last audited financial year ended more than six months before the date of the offer document.

Regulation 243: Objects and utilization of funds
(1) The offer document shall contain a clear statement of:
(a) Specific objects for which the issue proceeds will be utilized
(b) Amount proposed to be utilized for each object
(c) Schedule of deployment of funds
(d) Sources of funds if issue proceeds are insufficient to meet the objects
(e) Details of bridge financing, if any, obtained for the objects
(2) If the objects include investment in another entity, full details of that entity shall be provided.
(3) If the objects include acquisitions, terms of the acquisition shall be disclosed.
(4) A statement shall be included regarding deployment of funds in the event of oversubscription.

Regulation 244: Corporate governance disclosures
(1) The offer document shall confirm compliance with the following corporate governance requirements:
(a) Composition of Board of Directors including independent directors
(b) Constitution of Audit Committee
(c) Constitution of Nomination and Remuneration Committee
(d) Constitution of Stakeholders Relationship Committee
(e) Code of conduct for directors and senior management
(f) Vigil mechanism/whistleblower policy
(g) Related party transaction policy
(h) Risk management framework

Regulation 245: Declarations in offer document
(1) The offer document shall include the following declarations:
(a) Declaration by the Board of Directors that all information in the offer document is true, fair and correct and nothing material has been omitted
(b) Declaration by the statutory auditors that the financial information is true and accurate
(c) Declaration by the lead manager that due diligence has been exercised
(d) Declaration by the company secretary/compliance officer regarding compliance
(e) Declaration by each director regarding disqualifications under Companies Act, 2013

================================================================================
SCHEDULE III — REQUIREMENTS FOR RESTATED FINANCIAL STATEMENTS
================================================================================

1. Restated financial statements shall be prepared by adjusting the audited financial statements for:
(a) Changes in accounting policies adopted during the period under consideration
(b) Prior period items
(c) Extra-ordinary items
(d) Adjustments for qualifications in audit reports
(e) Other material adjustments

2. Restatement adjustments shall be made retrospectively by restating the figures of earlier years.

3. The notes to the restated financial statements shall include:
(a) Statement of adjustments made
(b) Significant accounting policies
(c) Segment reporting (if applicable)
(d) Related party disclosures
(e) Earnings per share computation
(f) Deferred tax assets/liabilities details

4. The restated financial statements shall include:
(a) Restated balance sheet
(b) Restated statement of profit and loss
(c) Restated cash flow statement
(d) Restated statement of changes in equity (for Ind AS companies)
(e) Notes to the restated financial statements

SCHEDULE IV — MINIMUM DISCLOSURES IN OFFER DOCUMENT
================================================================================

Part A — Cover Page of Offer Document:
1. Type of issue (Initial Public Offer)
2. Name of the issuer in full and its logo
3. Registered office address with telephone, fax, email and website
4. Name and address of the compliance officer
5. Name, address and telephone of the lead manager
6. Name, address and telephone of the registrar to the issue
7. Name of the SME exchange where the securities will be listed
8. Price band/issue price
9. Issue opens on: [Date]
10. Issue closes on: [Date]
11. Payment mode: All applicants to use ASBA facility
12. The securities are being issued through the Book Building Process / Fixed Price Process

Part B — Table of Contents (Mandatory Sections):
Section 1: General Information
Section 2: Risk Factors
Section 3: Introduction
Section 4: About the Company
Section 5: Financial Statements
Section 6: Legal and Other Information
Section 7: Other Regulatory and Statutory Disclosures
Section 8: Offering Information
Section 9: Material Contracts and Documents for Inspection
Section 10: Declaration

Part C — Mandatory Annexures:
Annexure 1: Restated Financial Statements
Annexure 2: Statement of Tax Benefits
Annexure 3: Details of Group Companies
Annexure 4: Promoters' Contribution Certificate
Annexure 5: Underwriting Agreement (if applicable)
Annexure 6: Market Maker Agreement
Annexure 7: Certificates from Statutory Auditors
