SECTION 1: EXECUTIVE SUMMARY & VISION
================================================================================
The preparation of a Draft Red Herring Prospectus (DRHP) for a Small and Medium
Enterprise (SME) Initial Public Offering (IPO) is traditionally an arduous, highly
manual process taking between 6 to 12 weeks. Merchant bankers, compliance officers,
company secretaries, and SMEs must navigate hundreds of pages of complex SEBI
Issue of Capital and Disclosure Requirements (ICDR) Regulations, Listing Obligations
and Disclosure Requirements (LODR), and continuous SEBI circular updates.

IPO Copilot AI transforms this workflow by introducing an enterprise-grade,
Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) platform that reduces document preparation
and regulatory compliance checking from weeks to days. Grounded strictly in SEBI
regulations and specific workspace documents, the system eliminates AI hallucinations
and provides full explainability for every recommendation.

Key Strategic Capabilities:
- Automated DRHP & Document Intelligence: Ingests financial statements, board
  resolutions, and background files using PyMuPDF and pdfplumber, chunking them
  into semantic sections with exact metadata preservation.
- Real-Time ICDR Compliance Engine: Runs automated rule-by-rule checks against
  SEBI ICDR 2018 regulations, classifying sections into Pass, Warning, or Fail
  with specific regulatory citations.
- Evidence Rail & Explainable AI: Every AI response provides exact traceability—
  displaying "Why it matters", "AI Reasoning", "Confidence Scores", and direct
  text quotes from source documents.
- Persistent & Floating SEBI Advisor (Copilot): A multi-turn conversational AI
  widget that streams answers via Server-Sent Events (SSE), persists history across
  sessions, and prioritizes workspace files over the general SEBI corpus.
- Draft Review & Section Generator: Performs section-level gap analysis, highlights
  omissions, and generates formal legal drafts for mandatory DRHP sections (e.g.,
  Risk Factors, Objects of Issue, Basis for Issue Price).

SECTION 4: END-TO-END WORKFLOW & USER JOURNEY
================================================================================
Stage 1: Authentication & Workspace Tenancy
- Users register and authenticate via secure JWT (`/api/v1/auth/login`).
- Upon login, the user selects or creates a dedicated Workspace (e.g., "Acme Robotics
  SME IPO"). Every subsequent operation is strictly scoped to `workspace_id`.

Stage 2: Document Ingestion & Chunking
- Users upload source PDFs (DRHP drafts, audited financials, board resolutions) via
  `/api/v1/workspaces/{id}/documents`.
- The backend saves files to `UPLOAD_DIR`, extracts text via PyMuPDF, divides content
  into 1000-character semantic chunks (with 150-char overlap), and indexes them into
  both PostgreSQL (`document_chunks`) and ChromaDB vector store (`get_workspace_vectorstore`).

Stage 3: Automated SEBI Compliance Checking
- Triggered via `/api/v1/workspaces/{id}/compliance/check`.
- The engine loads the SEBI SME Checklist (`sebi_rules.json`), retrieves relevant
  document chunks via vector similarity, and prompts the LLM to verify compliance.
- Output: Status (`pass`, `warning`, `fail`), Exact Quote Evidence, Regulatory
  Reference, and Confidence Score.

Stage 4: Draft Review & Gap Analysis
- Users submit specific DRHP sections (e.g., "Risk Factors") for deep review via
  `/api/v1/workspaces/{id}/reviews`.
- The AI compares text against mandatory SEBI disclosures, highlighting missing
  sub-clauses, vague risk phrasing, and providing drop-in replacement suggestions.

Stage 5: Professional DRHP Chart & Visual Analytics Generation
- In the DRHP Generator (`/api/v1/workspaces/{id}/drhp/generate`), the backend computes
  and attaches 3 professional visual analytics modules via `drhp_charts.py`:
  1. Peer Comparison Bar Charts (P/E, RoNW, EBITDA vs listed peers)
  2. Funds Utilization Donut Charts (Objects of Issue breakdown)
  3. Revenue & PAT CAGR Trajectory Line Charts

Stage 6: Persistent Copilot Chat & Evidence Rail
- Users interact with the floating `FloatingCopilot` widget across any page or
  navigate to the full SEBI Advisor page.
- When a query is asked, the `rag_query_full` pipeline searches workspace documents
  first, supplements with SEBI regulations, and streams tokens via Server-Sent
  Events (`SSE`). The Evidence Rail displays full explainability citations.

Stage 7: Audit Log Traceability & Export
- All user actions (document uploads, compliance runs, AI prompts, status updates)
  are cryptographically hashed and logged to `audit_events`.
- Users export final validation reports and audit logs as downloadable PDFs or
  Excel summaries via `/api/v1/workspaces/{id}/export`.

SECTION 5: CORE AI & RAG PIPELINE SPECIFICATIONS
================================================================================
5.1 Multi-Tier RAG Routing (`rag_pipeline.py`)
To ensure high precision and eliminate generic advice, the `rag_query_full()` engine
implements a strict 2-tier retrieval prioritization:
1. Workspace Vector Store Priority: Queries `get_workspace_vectorstore(workspace_id)`
   using `top_k=5`. If specific company documents contain relevant chunks, they are
   placed at the top of the context prompt.
2. SEBI Corpus Supplementation: Queries `get_sebi_vectorstore()`. If workspace
   documents were found, SEBI retrieval is dynamically tuned to `top_k=1` (purely
   for regulatory backing). If no workspace documents match, SEBI retrieval expands
   to `top_k=5` using Maximal Marginal Relevance (`MMR`, `fetch_k=15`) to ensure
   diverse clause coverage.

5.2 SEBI 2026 Corpus & Amendments Integration (`sebi_corpus/`)
The AI engine is pre-indexed with a curated repository of capital markets regulations,
verified up to Q1-Q2 2026 (`v2.1.0`):
- `icdr_regulations.txt` (28.0 KB): SEBI (ICDR) Regulations, 2018 — Chapter IX SME
  IPO eligibility, lock-in rules, promoter contribution, and pricing mandates.
- `lodr_regulations.txt` (19.7 KB): SEBI (LODR) Regulations, 2015 — Corporate
  governance, audit committee composition, and mandatory disclosures.
- `sebi_2026_amendments.txt` (17.7 KB): Latest 2026 SEBI Circulars: Digital DRHP
  filings via PRISM portal, mandatory AI-generated content disclosure, UPI-only ASBA
  limits, Green IPO sustainability frameworks, and GIFT City dual-listing norms.
- `sme_ipo_guidelines.txt` (18.5 KB): NSE Emerge and BSE SME specific listing
  criteria, post-issue capital thresholds (min Rs. 1 crore, max Rs. 25 crore).
- `drhp_*_factors.txt` (42.2 KB total): Standardized templates and risk factor
  taxonomies for Business Overview, Risk Factors, and Financial Statements.

5.3 Anti-Hallucination & Explainability Guardrails
- System Guard & Prompt Sanitization (`SYSTEM_GUARD`): If retrieved context docs
  are empty, the prompt explicitly instructs the LLM: *"If the context does not
  contain the answer, you MUST return EXACTLY: No supporting evidence found. Do not
  hallucinate or guess."*
- Evidence Rail UI (`EvidenceRail.tsx`): Every compliance check or chat answer
  parses the LLM output into distinct visual compartments: Why it matters (regulatory
  impact), AI Reasoning (deductive logic applied), and Supporting Citations (exact
  verbatim text quotes from page X of document Y).
- Offline-Resilient Embedding Router (`model_router.py`): Hardcodes/prefers local
  `sentence-transformers/all-MiniLM-L6-v2` (`local_minilm`) for blazing-fast vector
  embeddings directly inside the backend container without cloud rate limits.

SECTION 6: DATABASE & DATA MODEL ARCHITECTURE
================================================================================
1. `User` (`users`)
   - Attributes: `id` (PK), `email`, `hashed_password`, `full_name`, `role`, `is_active`

2. `Workspace` (`workspaces`)
   - Attributes: `id` (PK), `name`, `description`, `user_id` (FK -> users.id), `created_at`
   - Purpose: The core multi-tenant boundary. Every document, compliance check,

   - Attributes: `id` (PK), `workspace_id` (FK), `name`, `cin`, `sector`,
     `incorporation_date`, `paid_up_capital`
   - Purpose: Corporate profile and capital metrics used to auto-evaluate SME eligibility.

4. `Document` (`documents`)
   - Attributes: `id` (PK), `workspace_id` (FK), `filename`, `file_path`, `file_size`,
     `doc_type`, `version`, `status`
   - Purpose: Tracks uploaded files with automated version incrementing.

5. `DocumentChunk` (`document_chunks`)
   - Attributes: `id` (PK), `document_id` (FK), `chunk_index`, `content`,
     `page_number`, `chroma_id`
   - Purpose: Stores semantic text segments in PostgreSQL alongside mapping IDs to ChromaDB.

6. `ComplianceCheck` (`compliance_checks`)
   - Attributes: `id` (PK), `workspace_id` (FK), `rule_id`, `rule_name`, `status`,
     `evidence`, `why`, `confidence`
   - Purpose: Records the structured JSON output of automated SEBI ICDR evaluations.

7. `CopilotThread` & `CopilotMessage`
   - Attributes: `id` (PK), `workspace_id` (FK), `thread_id` (FK), `role` (`user`/`assistant`),

   - Purpose: Persists multi-turn conversational histories across sessions.

8. `Review` (`reviews`)
   - Attributes: `id` (PK), `workspace_id` (FK), `document_id` (FK), `section_name`,
     `status`, `comments_json`
   - Purpose: Stores section-level draft reviews, AI suggestions, and human sign-offs.

9. `AuditEvent` (`audit_events`)
   - Attributes: `id` (PK), `workspace_id` (FK), `user_id` (FK), `action`,
     `resource_type`, `details`, `ip_address`
   - Purpose: Immutable trail of all regulatory actions, logins, and document modifications.

SECTION 7: SECURITY & PRODUCTION HARDENING
================================================================================
7.1 Strict Workspace Tenancy Isolation
Every API router (`documents.py`, `compliance.py`, `copilot.py`, `reviews.py`,
`drhp.py`) enforces explicit workspace ownership validation using authenticated JWT:
```python
workspace = await db.scalar(
    select(Workspace).where(
        Workspace.id == workspace_id,
        Workspace.user_id == current_user.id
    )
)
```
If the query returns `None`, the API immediately raises HTTP `404 Not Found` or `403 Forbidden`,
ensuring users can never enumerate or access documents belonging to other SMEs.

7.2 Path Traversal & File System Protections
File upload (`/workspaces/{id}/documents/upload`) and download endpoints strictly
sanitize incoming filenames and verify absolute directory boundaries:
- Filenames are stripped of path components using `os.path.basename(file.filename)`
  and validated against allowed extensions (`.pdf`, `.docx`, `.txt`, `.xlsx`).
- Target paths are resolved using `Path(upload_path).resolve()` and asserted to start
  with `Path(settings.UPLOAD_DIR).resolve()`, preventing directory traversal attacks.

7.3 API Rate Limiting & Global Exception Handling
- Rate Limiting (`slowapi`): High-cost AI generation and authentication endpoints
  are throttled (`POST /workspaces/{id}/copilot/chat` is protected by `@limiter.limit('30/minute')`).
- Global Exception Handlers (`main.py`): Custom async exception handlers intercept
  `SQLAlchemyError`, `asyncio.TimeoutError`, and unhandled `Exception` instances.
  Instead of exposing internal stack traces or SQL schemas, the API returns standardized,
  clean JSON responses: `{"success": False, "error": {"code": "DATABASE_ERROR", "message": "..."}}`.

SECTION 8: FRONTEND ARCHITECTURE & DESIGN SYSTEM
================================================================================
8.1 State Management Architecture
- TanStack Query v5 (Server State): All asynchronous backend queries (`useWorkspaces`,
  `useDocuments`, `useComplianceChecks`) use TanStack Query with global
  `staleTime: 5 * 60 * 1000` (5 minutes) and `gcTime: 30 * 60 * 1000` (30 minutes).
- Zustand Stores (Client State): `useAuthStore` manages JWT tokens (`access_token`)
  and current user profiles. `useWorkspaceStore` tracks `currentWorkspaceId` and
  `currentWorkspaceName` across all route transitions.
- Chat Context (`ChatContext.tsx`): Manages multi-thread conversational state,
  persisting local chat backups to `localStorage` while synchronizing with backend tables.

8.2 Key Interactive UI Components
- `FloatingCopilot.tsx` (Persistent ChatGPT Widget): Mounted globally inside `AppShell.tsx`,
  this floating blue widget stays visible across all pages without unmounting during
  route navigation. Users can expand/collapse the panel, switch chat threads, and
  stream responses with real-time typing indicators.
- `ReadinessGauge.tsx` (SME IPO Readiness Score): Renders an interactive Recharts
  radial gauge on the dashboard, computing a composite 0-100 score based on uploaded
  documents and compliance check pass rates ("Ready to File" >=80, "In Progress" >=60).
- `EvidenceRail.tsx` & `ComplianceCheckRow.tsx`: Collapsible cards displaying
  compliance statuses (`Pass ▲`, `Review ■`, `Critical ▲`), exact quotes, and citations.

SECTION 10: KNOWN LIMITATIONS & PRODUCTION ROADMAP
================================================================================
1. Object Storage Migration (AWS S3 / Azure Blob):
   Currently, uploaded files reside on the local disk (`UPLOAD_DIR`). For multi-node
   Kubernetes clusters, file storage must migrate to S3 to ensure load-balanced
   instances can access uploaded PDFs uniformly.

2. Distributed Vector Store Scaling:
   While local persistent ChromaDB performs exceptionally well for SME IPO corpora
   (~100,000 chunks), enterprise SaaS deployments handling thousands of concurrent
   merchant bankers should scale to distributed vector stores such as Pinecone,
   Qdrant, or Milvus.

3. Specialized OCR for Dense Financial Tables:
   Standard PDF extraction (`fitz`/`pdfplumber`) extracts text reliably but may
   flatten highly complex, multi-page audited financial tables. Integrating tabular
   OCR (e.g., Amazon Textract or specialized LLM vision parsers) will further elevate
   numerical precision during balance sheet cross-checking.

4. WebSocket Fallback for SSE:
   Server-Sent Events (`SSE`) power our streaming chat effectively. However, certain
   strict corporate banking firewalls occasionally drop idle HTTP streams; implementing
   a dual SSE/WebSocket adapter will maximize connection resilience.
